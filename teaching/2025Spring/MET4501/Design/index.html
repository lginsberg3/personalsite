Machine Design [MET 4501]

Module 3: Design of Mechanical Elements

Module 3 Overview

In this module, we will discuss the design of various mechanical elements used in machines, including:

  • Chapter 7: Shafts and Shaft Components (Lecture 16)
    • Shaft design
    • Critical speeds for shafts
    • Miscellaneous shaft components
    • Limits and fits

  • Chapter 10: Mechanical Springs (Lectures 17-18)
    • Stresses in helical springs
    • Spring materials
    • Critical frequency of helical springs
    • Fatigue of helical compression springs

  • Chapter 11: Rolling-Contact Bearings (Lectures 19-20)
    • Bearing types
    • Relating bearing load, life, and reliability
    • Combined radial and thrust loading

  • Chapter 12: Lubrication and Journal Bearings (Lecture 21)
    • Types of lubrication
    • Petroff's equation
    • Design variables

  • Chapter 13: Gears - General (Lectures 22-23)
    • Types of gears
    • Contact ratio
    • Gear trains
    • Force analysis

  • Chapter 14: Spur and Helical Gears (Lecture 24)
    • The Lewis bending equation
    • Surface durability

  • Chapter 8: Screws, Fasteners, and the Design of Nonpermanent Joints (Lecture 25)
    • Thread standards and definitions
    • Joint stiffness
    • Bolt strength
    • Relating bolt torque to bolt tension

At the end of this module, you will be equipped with essential tools to design and analyze machine components.
